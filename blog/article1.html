The Science of Studying: How to Retain 90% of What You Read

EduOdia Team

Nov 18, 2025 • 5 min read

In this Article

  • • Why passive reading doesn't work
  • • The Pomodoro Technique explained
  • • How to use Active Recall for exams

Have you ever spent three hours reading a textbook, only to realize you can’t remember a single thing you just read? You are not alone. Most students in Odisha fall into the trap of "Passive Studying"—simply moving their eyes over the text without engaging their brain. To score high marks in your Board exams, you need to shift your strategy from working hard to working smart.

1. The Illusion of Competence

When we highlight lines in a book or re-read notes, our brain recognizes the text and tells us, "I know this." This is a lie. Recognizing text is not the same as being able to recall it. Real learning happens when you close the book and try to explain the concept to yourself. If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough.

"Stop treating your brain like a hard drive to store data. Treat it like a muscle that needs to be exercised through testing."

2. The Power of Active Recall

Active Recall is the single most effective study technique proven by science. Instead of reading a chapter four times, read it once, then close the book and write down everything you remember. This struggle to "retrieve" information strengthens the neural pathways in your brain. It feels difficult and tiring, but that is exactly why it works.

3. Use the Pomodoro Technique

Our focus works like a battery; it drains over time. The Pomodoro Technique suggests studying in focused bursts. Set a timer for 25 minutes of intense focus, followed by a 5-minute break. After four cycles, take a longer break. This prevents burnout and keeps your mind fresh, allowing you to study for longer periods without getting exhausted.

4. Creating the Right Environment

Your environment dictates your focus. If your phone is on your desk, your IQ drops by 10 points because part of your brain is constantly waiting for a notification. Put your phone in another room. Keep your desk clean. When you sit down, your brain should know it is time to work. Small changes in your physical space can lead to massive improvements in your marks.

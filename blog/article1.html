How to Score 90% in Board Exams

By EduOdia Team • Nov 18, 2025

Have you ever spent three hours reading a textbook, only to realize you can’t remember a single thing? You are not alone. Most students fall into the trap of "Passive Studying". To score high marks, you need to work smart.

1. The Illusion of Competence

When we highlight lines in a book, our brain tells us, "I know this." This is a lie. Real learning happens when you close the book and try to explain the concept to yourself.

Pro Tip: Don't just read. Test yourself. If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough.

2. Active Recall

Active Recall is the single most effective study technique. Instead of reading a chapter four times, read it once, then close the book and write down everything you remember.

3. The Pomodoro Technique

Study in focused bursts. Set a timer for 25 minutes of intense work, followed by a 5-minute break. This keeps your brain fresh.
